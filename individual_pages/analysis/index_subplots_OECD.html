The following graphs show the relationships between a selection of metrics and the number of COVID-19 deaths per million. For each metric, all OECD countries are included for which data are available. Each plot includes an OLS regression line, along with its formula and R2 value. A description of the metrics and their sources can be found below the graphs. Deaths statistics are from JHU CSSE COVID-19 Data.



Metric Description Source
% Overweight The percentage of adults aged 18 years or over in a country with a BMI of over 25. World Health Organization
Median Age Median age of the country's population. The World Factbook - CIA
% Single Household The percentage of households with a single resident. United Nations
People per House The mean number of usual residents per household. United Nations
% Living in Urban Area The percentage of the population living in urban areas. The World Bank
Average Annual Cigarettes per Person The mean number of legally sold cigarettes smoked per person aged 15 or over per year. Tobacco Atlas
Democracy Score Value representing the state of democracy in a country, ranging from 0, most authoritarian, to 10, most democratic. The Economist Intelligence Unit
Healthcare as % of GDP Total expenditure on healthcare as a percentage of GDP. Note this is both private and public expenditure. The World Bank
PM2.5 Mean annual exposure to PM2.5 air pollution particles. The World Bank
Gini Coefficient The Gini coefficient is a measure of income inequality in a country. The higher the number the, the higher the level of income inequality. The World Bank
Political Trust Public trust in politicians on a scale 1-7, where 1 is the lowest level of trust and 7 is the highest. The World Bank
GHS Overall Score Combined measure of a country's ability to prevent, detect and respond to emerging pathogens. It also includes the robustness of the health service, committment to international norms of health practice, and the country's vulnerability to biological threats. A more detailed description of the index can be found on their website. Global Health Security Index
